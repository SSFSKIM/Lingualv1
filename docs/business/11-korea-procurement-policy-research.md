# Korea Procurement And Policy Research

Status: Research draft
Last updated: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This document summarizes what Lingual needs to understand before pursuing Korean public elementary and middle school adoption for English speaking practice.

It focuses on business, policy, procurement, privacy, pilot, and buyer-path questions. It is not legal advice and should not be used as a final compliance checklist.

## Research Scope

- Korean public elementary and middle schools.
- English-class adoption.
- District/government buyer path.
- AI Digital Textbook policy and adjacent channels.
- Public procurement and education-office sales motion.
- Privacy and minor-data considerations.
- Pilot and reporting norms.
- Direct school-facing competitor paths, especially Plang School.
- Practical implications for a small or foreign vendor.

## Executive Summary

Korea is not a blank market for AI English speaking. The strongest signal from the second-pass research is that Korean buyers may not frame the category as only "AI speaking practice." The school-facing frame is more likely to include:

- English process-centered assessment.
- Performance assessment support.
- Teacher workload reduction.
- Student activity evidence.
- Student-record comments and class documentation.
- Integration with education-office platforms or school workflows.

Plang School is the clearest warning signal. It is explicitly school-facing, appears to have education-office supply signals, and positions itself around English process assessment, not just consumer AI fluency practice. This makes Plang a Tier 1 Korean competitor for Lingual's public-school strategy.

For Lingual, the practical Korea wedge should not be "AI English speaking app." A stronger wedge is:

> Teacher-designed conversational practice tied to curriculum objectives, with privacy-safe deployment, speaking-time evidence, and pilot reports that education offices and school administrators can use.

## Confirmed Market / Policy Facts

### AI Digital Textbook Is A Strategic Backdrop

The Korean government announced AI Digital Textbook rollout beginning with subjects including English, math, information, and special-education Korean in 2025, with private publishers and edtech companies expected to develop textbook products. The policy goal includes student-data-based personalized learning and cloud-based delivery. Source: [Korea policy briefing on AI Digital Textbooks](https://www.korea.kr/news/policyNewsView.do?newsId=148916075).

Business interpretation:

- AI English products are entering a policy environment where public schools, education offices, publishers, and edtech vendors already expect digital learning data.
- Textbook-linked vendors have a built-in channel advantage.
- Lingual should avoid positioning itself as a replacement for AI Digital Textbooks. It should position as a speaking-practice and pilot-evidence layer that can work with curricula or textbooks.

### AI Digital Textbook Usage Creates Operational Friction

A Gyeonggi Office of Education document describes AI Digital Textbook usage steps involving Digital Onepass signup, AIDT portal login/book creation, NEIS-linked school information, and required personal-information consent. It also describes education-office negotiations with publishers around unused accounts and usage-fee adjustment. Source: [Gyeonggi Office of Education AIDT usage-fee case PDF](https://www.goe.go.kr/resource/goe/na/bbs_1916/2025/12/58b88f3c-bc30-45f4-b428-7798d2511bfc.pdf).

Business interpretation:

- Public-school digital learning adoption can fail operationally even after purchase if accounts, consent, login, and usage tracking are not clean.
- Education offices care about whether paid licenses are actually used.
- Lingual's pilot reporting should include activation and usage evidence, not only teacher satisfaction.

### Digital Onepass And Consent Matter

School communications around Digital Onepass show that students may need school-supported signup, personal-information collection/use consent, and legal-representative handling for students under 14 in some cases. Source: [Gwangyang Hitech High School Digital Onepass notice PDF](https://gyh.hs.jne.kr/data/attach_data/goksung/gwang-yangsilgo_hs/k2board/na/bbs_71057/ntt_1800526308/doc_3bcbv144c%3D2av24%3D4dvec%3D89v94%3D3ef2v6e56v49b9_v8387.pdf).

Business interpretation:

- Korea public-school pilots should assume identity, consent, and student-data handling are central implementation issues.
- Student under-14 handling needs Korean legal review before elementary/middle-school pilots.
- Lingual should prepare Korean-language privacy and consent materials before serious public-school pilots.

### CSAP May Matter For Public-Sector Cloud

KISA describes the Cloud Security Assurance Program as a cloud-service security certification system for cloud services, including SaaS, provided for national/public-agency business. Source: [KISA CSAP overview](https://www.kisa.kr/1050603).

Business interpretation:

- Lingual should not assume a normal consumer SaaS deployment will be acceptable for Korean public-sector procurement.
- Whether CSAP is required depends on the exact buyer path, deployment model, hosting, and public-sector interpretation. This needs dedicated legal/procurement research.
- A local partner, public-cloud deployment path, or education-office platform integration may become commercially important.

## Plang School Findings

Plang has a dedicated school product, Plang School / 플랭스쿨, positioned around "English process-centered assessment" and "AI-supported class and assessment." Its school page covers writing, reading, speaking, and listening; teacher and student login; "free start"; and adoption inquiry flows. It emphasizes AI + teacher feedback, handwriting scoring, real-time quizzes, AI reading scaffolding, speaking pronunciation/content feedback, listening assessment, and student activity evidence for Korean student-record-style comments. Source: [Plang School](https://edu.plang.ai/school).

Plang's consumer product is also a strong AI English-speaking app. It claims personalized AI curriculum, real-time sentence analysis, native-video/shadowing practice, speaking feedback, and 100K+ Google Play downloads. Source: [Plang Google Play listing](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=co.aienglish). Plang's AI technology page describes corpus/pattern analysis, learner-level matching, and LLM + learner-data RAG for personalized expression and utterance feedback. Source: [Plang AI technology](https://www.plang.ai/ai-technology).

Adoption signals:

- A Seoul education news report says Seoul Metropolitan Office of Education signed a second-year supply contract for Plang School in March 2026 after first-year adoption, citing positive teacher response and fit for English speaking/writing assessment. Source: [Daehak Journal / DH News](https://m.dhnews.co.kr/news/view/1065573661439195).
- A Jeonnam report says Jeollanam-do Office of Education signed a supply contract for Plang School, connected it to Jeonnam's `아이모두` platform, and planned to distribute it free to all Jeonnam schools during 2026. Source: [BeyondPost](https://www.beyondpost.co.kr/view.php?ud=2026050418260366779aeda69934_30).
- A Gyeonggi elementary school privacy policy lists `㈜플랭` among providers receiving student data for `하이러닝` service use, including name, school, grade, class, number, email, password, submitted answers, and solving time, retained for one year through the school year. Source: [Dogung Elementary privacy policy](https://dogung-e.goegh.kr/dogung-e/iv/indvdlView/selectIndvdlView.do).

Business interpretation:

- Plang is a direct Korean school competitor, not only a consumer app.
- Its likely playbook is education-office-led adoption, platform linkage, and free/no-friction school access during the funded period.
- It understands Korean English-teacher workflows: process assessment, performance assessment, teacher rubrics, student evidence, and student-record comments.
- Lingual should treat "process assessment" as required Korean buyer vocabulary, but not reduce its whole positioning to assessment automation.

## Buyer And Procurement Path

Likely public-school buyer paths:

1. **Education-office-led adoption.**
   Product is selected or contracted by a metropolitan/provincial office of education and then distributed to schools.

2. **Platform-linked adoption.**
   Product enters through an existing education-office platform, digital learning system, AI courseware initiative, or school learning platform.

3. **Publisher / AI Digital Textbook channel.**
   Product is bundled with textbook, curriculum, or publisher workflows.

4. **School-level pilot.**
   A teacher or department starts usage, but public-school student-data handling and procurement may still require school or education-office approval.

5. **Procurement portal / local partner path.**
   S2B / school-market style channels may matter for certain purchases, but the exact fit for Lingual's AI speaking SaaS needs validation. Source reference: [S2B school market](https://www.s2b.kr/).

Business interpretation:

- For Korea public schools, Lingual should assume the final scalable buyer is not the individual teacher.
- The most realistic long-term path may require an education-office pilot, Korean partner, publisher relationship, or platform integration.
- A first school pilot can still be useful, but it should be designed to produce evidence that an education office can evaluate.

## AI Digital Textbook Implications

AI Digital Textbook is not the same category as Lingual, but it shapes buyer expectations.

Relevant expectations:

- Student data and learning analytics.
- School-approved login and identity flows.
- Teacher dashboards and learning evidence.
- Publisher and curriculum linkage.
- Privacy consent and data-sharing visibility.
- Usage reporting tied to budget execution.

Lingual implication:

- Lingual should position itself as an AI speaking-practice layer that can support teacher-created or school-selected curriculum objectives.
- The product should export pilot evidence in a form administrators can understand.
- Lingual should not claim it is an AI Digital Textbook unless it enters that regulatory/publisher channel.

## Privacy, Minor Data, And Legal Friction

Likely questions for Korean public-school pilots:

- What personal data is collected?
- Are names, grade, class, student number, email, submitted answers, usage logs, transcripts, or voice/audio stored?
- Is raw audio stored, or only transcripts/derived feedback?
- Where is data hosted?
- Are overseas subprocessors used?
- Is student data transferred overseas?
- What is the retention period?
- How can schools delete data after pilot?
- What happens for students under 14?
- Is parent/legal representative consent required?
- Does the product require Digital Onepass, school account creation, or a separate login?
- Does the product need CSAP, ISMS-P, local hosting, or public cloud certification for a public buyer?

Business interpretation:

- Korean-language privacy materials are not optional for serious Korea public-school work.
- Audio/transcript policy should be prepared early.
- Legal/procurement research should happen before promising school or education-office pilots.

## Pilot And Reporting Implications

Korean pilot reporting should likely include:

- Teacher satisfaction.
- Student satisfaction.
- Speaking time and usage yield.
- Average session length.
- Class-level activation.
- Teacher workload impact.
- Sample AI debriefs.
- Evidence that maps to curriculum objectives.
- Evidence that can support process-centered assessment.
- Implementation friction: login, consent, setup, support, device/network constraints.

Business interpretation:

- Lingual's pilot report should not only say students practiced more.
- It should show how the teacher can use the practice evidence in class assessment and reporting.
- This is where Lingual can compete with Plang without copying Plang's broader writing/reading/listening assessment suite.

## Implications For Lingual

Ready to use now:

- Founder-led school pilots.
- Teacher-selected class sections.
- Free first pilot.
- Survey-based reporting.
- Speaking-time and usage metrics.

Needs preparation before serious Korea public-school pilots:

- Korean-language privacy notice.
- Korean-language school pilot consent and data-use explanation.
- Audio/transcript retention and deletion policy.
- Subprocessor and overseas-transfer explanation.
- Korea-specific pilot report template.
- Process-assessment vocabulary mapping.
- Buyer-path strategy: education office, school, publisher, platform partner, or local reseller.

Should not be claimed yet:

- Korea public-school procurement readiness.
- Education-office-wide deployment readiness.
- AI Digital Textbook equivalence.
- Full Korean public-sector compliance.
- CSAP / ISMS-P readiness unless verified.

Likely strongest Korea positioning:

> Teacher-designed AI conversation assignments for English classes, aligned to curriculum objectives and producing class-ready speaking evidence for pilots and process-centered assessment.

- What student-data, cloud-hosting, parental-consent, and data-localization requirements would apply to AI speaking practice?
- What procurement route is realistic for a first pilot: direct school pilot, education-office pilot, publisher partnership, local partner, or research/university channel?
- What evidence does a Korean public buyer expect after a pilot?
- What does Plang School's education-office adoption signal imply about the most realistic buyer path for Lingual?
- Were Seoul and Jeonnam Plang contracts direct awards, open tenders, S2B/KONEPS purchases, pilot/service agreements, or platform-level procurements?
