# Product Overview

Status: Business v1 draft
Last updated: 2026-06-01

## One-line description

Lingual is an AI speaking-practice platform for schools that lets teachers design curriculum-aligned conversation assignments and lets students practice at scale with consent-aware voice or text support.

## What it is

Lingual is a school-focused language-learning product. Its core promise is:

> Teacher-designed practice, AI-executed at student scale.

The product is not positioned as a generic chatbot. The school beta is built around teacher control, class workflows, assignment-specific practice, measurable speaking activity, and school-safe consent handling.

## Segment priority

1. Independent and public high schools, primarily world-language classes.
2. Language institutes, likely with mixed language offerings and more flexible class structures.
3. ESL / ELD programs, English-first.
4. Colleges, later phase because the operational system and buying process are different.

## First outreach targets

- United States: independent and public high schools in the DC-Maryland-Virginia area, primarily world-language classes.
- Korea: public elementary and middle schools, focused on English-class adoption.
- Independent schools and language institutions: start with school administrators and language department leadership.
- Public schools: use pilot surveys and Lingual-compiled reports to support later district/government conversations.

## Problem being solved

Teachers do not have enough class time to give every student meaningful spoken practice. AI can increase speaking time, but most AI tools fail school needs:

- They are not controlled by the teacher's current lesson goals.
- They do not reliably target specific expressions, grammar, tasks, or rubrics.
- They produce generic usage summaries rather than instruction-changing signals.
- They create unanswered questions around voice consent, student data, retention, and parent/guardian workflows.
- They can become too expensive if every practice minute defaults to realtime voice.

## Core value proposition

1. **More speaking practice without more teacher minutes.**

   Teachers can assign structured speaking tasks that students complete outside limited class talk time.

2. **Teacher control over AI behavior.**

   Assignments can include scenario, objectives, target expressions, target vocabulary, focus grammar, success criteria, language-mix settings, and teacher notes.

3. **Practice tied to instructional evidence.**

   Teachers can see class, assignment, and student-level signals such as speaking time, self-corrections, repeated errors, target-expression usage, rubric evidence, and task completion.

4. **School-safe rollout posture.**

   Voice access is gated by consent state, text fallback can be used when voice is blocked, guardian consent packets exist, and admins can manage compliance state.

5. **LMS-aligned pilot motion.**

   Canvas course content, roster visibility, LTI launch, deep-link assignment embedding, and completion grade passback are part of the current school beta surface.

## Business positioning

Lingual should be positioned as:

> An AI speaking-practice platform for schools.

The more specific school-facing version is:

> A teacher-controlled AI speaking-practice layer for school language programs.

This is different from:

- A consumer language app focused on solo self-study.
- A generic AI tutor with weak curriculum boundaries.
- A simple pronunciation checker.
- A classroom LMS.
- A gradebook or full student information system.

The next market-positioning research should focus first on school- or institution-used AI-powered conversation-practice platforms, especially B2B products where realtime conversation, speaking practice, or listening practice is central. Broader comparisons against language labs, LMS add-ons, AI tutors, assessment tools, or consumer language apps can be secondary.

The strongest school-facing message is that Lingual combines teacher intent, AI conversation, structured speaking evidence, and compliance-aware rollout controls.

## What business should not overclaim

- Do not claim certified assessment scoring.
- Do not claim full FERPA/COPPA/BIPA compliance until counsel validates the workflows.
- Do not claim district-scale procurement readiness.
- Do not claim Google Classroom support as current.
- Do not claim enterprise SSO, billing controls, audit-log completeness, or SIS-grade integration.
- Do not claim teachers can currently review full student transcripts or play back voice recordings.

## Legal Research Priority

The main legal/procurement research scope is Korean public schools and US public schools. Independent-school legal review still matters, but it is not the primary research priority for the next phase.
