# Product Tear-Down Research Plan

Status: Research plan
Last updated: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This document defines the next research pass after the market and positioning scan.

The prior research is sufficient for market positioning, but not yet sufficient for product roadmap decisions. This pass should examine how priority competitors actually work for teachers and students.

The main question is:

> Which competitor workflows already cover the product experience Lingual wants to own, and where is there still an open product gap?

## Primary Research Questions

For each priority competitor, research four workflow areas:

1. **Teacher assignment creation.**
   How does a teacher create, configure, assign, or customize speaking/conversation practice?

2. **Student conversation experience.**
   What does the student actually do? Is the conversation realtime, async, avatar/video, text-first, voice-first, recording-based, chatbot-based, or exercise-based? How natural does it appear?

3. **Feedback / debrief output.**
   What does the student receive after practice? What does the teacher receive? Is the output a score, transcript, pronunciation mark, rubric, AI summary, correction, recommendation, or reusable class evidence?

4. **Teacher dashboard usefulness.**
   Can the dashboard help a teacher make class decisions? Does it show class progress, student-level evidence, speaking time, completion, rubric trends, curriculum alignment, risk flags, or exportable reports?

## Evidence Standard

Use stronger evidence where possible:

| Evidence type | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Product demo video | Strongest public evidence for actual UX. |
| Help center / docs | Strong evidence for workflows and setup. |
| Screenshots on official site | Useful but may be marketing-selected. |
| Teacher onboarding guide | Strong evidence for classroom workflow. |
| Student guide | Strong evidence for student UX. |
| Admin / integration docs | Strong evidence for school readiness. |
| Case study | Useful for adoption and outcomes, weaker for actual UX. |
| Marketing copy only | Weak evidence for product specifics. |
| App store listing only | Weak unless it includes concrete screenshots or data-handling details. |

If only marketing copy is available, mark the product area as `Need to be resolved`.

## Research Dimensions

Each competitor should be evaluated with this table:

| Dimension | What to capture |
| --- | --- |
| Assignment creation | Teacher workflow, configuration options, rubrics, prompts, scenarios, curriculum tags, student grouping, due dates. |
| Teacher control depth | Whether the teacher controls goals, target expressions, difficulty, speaking mode, assessment criteria, AI constraints, or feedback style. |
| Student conversation UX | Realtime/async, voice/text/video/avatar, turn-taking quality, scaffolding, hints, retry behavior, device requirements. |
| Feedback / debrief | Student-facing feedback, teacher-facing feedback, transcript, rubric, pronunciation, fluency, grammar, vocabulary, AI summary, recommended next steps. |
| Dashboard utility | Class view, student view, progress trends, completion, speaking time, average session length, standards/curriculum mapping, export/report. |
| Evidence artifact | Whether the product creates something a teacher can use for grading, process assessment, parent/admin reporting, or intervention. |
| Integration surface | LMS, Google Classroom, Canvas, Clever, ClassLink, LTI, roster import, school platform, education-office platform. |
| Setup friction | Teacher self-serve, school admin setup, education-office setup, account creation, consent, device/network requirements. |
| Procurement/compliance clue | Privacy docs, DPA, school data terms, public procurement signal, minor-data handling, cloud/security posture. |
| Product gap vs Lingual | Where Lingual can be meaningfully better or different. |

## Korea Priority Targets

### Plang School

Research focus:

- How teachers create English process-centered assessment tasks.
- Whether speaking tasks are open-ended conversation or structured response/feedback.
- How Plang produces student activity evidence and student-record comments.
- Whether teachers can customize scenarios, rubrics, curriculum objectives, or feedback criteria.
- Whether dashboard/reporting is teacher-only or school/admin-ready.
- What the Seoul/Jeonnam/Gyeonggi public-school signals reveal about rollout and workflow.

Why it matters:

Plang School is the highest-priority Korean product teardown because it appears closest to Korean English teacher workflows.

### LG CNS Speaking Class / AI Tutor

Research focus:

- How teachers create AI foreign-language conversation content.
- Whether student practice is realtime conversation, structured speaking, or scoring workflow.
- What feedback outputs are generated.
- Whether education-office deployments include teacher dashboards or admin reporting.
- What product screens, guide materials, or videos show about actual use.

Why it matters:

LG CNS is the strongest Korea public-sector infrastructure competitor.

### EBS AI PengTalk

Research focus:

- Student app flow for textbook/daily English speaking.
- Teacher role, if any, in assigning or reviewing student work.
- Feedback type: pronunciation, category scoring, conversation output, progress.
- Whether it is useful beyond elementary self-study.

Why it matters:

PengTalk sets the public elementary baseline and buyer expectation for free/public AI English speaking.

### YBM / Visang AI Digital Textbook Ecosystem

Research focus:

- How speaking/listening activities are embedded in textbook workflows.
- Teacher assignment and monitoring flow.
- Student login/setup friction.
- Dashboard/reporting and learning analytics.
- Whether AI speaking is deep enough to compete with Lingual or mostly adjacent.

Why it matters:

AI Digital Textbook vendors may not be direct speaking competitors in every case, but they shape school procurement, curriculum linkage, and platform expectations.

## United States Priority Targets

### Speakable

Research focus:

- Teacher activity creation and AI scoring setup.
- Rubric, proficiency benchmark, portfolio, and feedback workflow.
- Dashboard and class reporting.
- Integration depth with Google Classroom, Clever, Canvas/LTI, grade passback.

Why it matters:

Speakable is the strongest US benchmark for district-ready speaking/writing assessment and procurement clarity.

### Speakology AI

Research focus:

- Teacher setup of AI conversation/video-call tasks.
- Student oral-practice UX and avatar/video realism.
- Transcript, feedback, oral quiz, and gradebook outputs.
- LMS/Canvas workflow and teacher review.

Why it matters:

Speakology is closest to AI conversation practice for language class.

### Telo AI

Research focus:

- ELL teacher assignment workflow.
- Student voice practice via robot/web.
- WIDA/TELPAS/ELD alignment and reporting.
- Dashboard, rostering, and Clever/ClassLink flow.

Why it matters:

Telo is a strong ELL-specific comparator for school-managed speaking practice.

### Extempore

Research focus:

- Teacher setup of speaking assessments and live interpersonal tasks.
- Student recording/live conversation flow.
- Rubrics, grading, common assessments, department reporting.
- LMS and grade sync workflow.

Why it matters:

Extempore is a strong substitute for speaking assessment even if it is not full AI conversation practice.

### Nualang

Research focus:

- Teacher-created roleplay/chatbot scenarios.
- Student roleplay UX.
- AI feedback and teacher review.
- Wayside/curriculum integration and classroom progress.

Why it matters:

Nualang is a relevant world-language roleplay and chatbot benchmark.

### ELSA Schools

Research focus:

- Assignment and lesson setup for schools.
- Student speaking/pronunciation UX.
- Pronunciation/fluency feedback details.
- Teacher dashboard and class reporting.

Why it matters:

ELSA is the mature pronunciation/speaking engine benchmark. Lingual should know what table-stakes speech feedback looks like.

## Early Hypotheses To Test

1. **Korea competitors may be stronger at assessment evidence than live conversation design.**
   Plang and AIDT vendors appear strong around process assessment, feedback, and student evidence, but teacher-authored open-ended conversation depth needs verification.

2. **US competitors may be stronger at procurement workflow than conversation depth.**
   Speakable and Extempore appear strong on school workflows, LMS, and reporting, but may leave room for richer realtime conversation.

3. **Pronunciation feedback is table stakes.**
   ELSA, Plang, LG CNS, and others already cover speech feedback. Lingual should not treat pronunciation scoring as a primary differentiator.

4. **The open product gap may be practice-to-evidence continuity.**
   Lingual's opportunity is likely the end-to-end chain: teacher objective -> adaptive conversation -> student debrief -> teacher evidence -> pilot report.

## Research Notes

- Use official docs, demo videos, help centers, product screenshots, school guides, and public onboarding materials first.
- Do not infer mature workflows from marketing language alone.
- Separate teacher UX from student UX.
- Separate student feedback from teacher dashboard evidence.
- Mark inaccessible demo-only details as `Need to be resolved`.
