# GTM Readiness

Status: Business v1 draft
Last updated: 2026-06-01

## Executive read

Lingual is ready for controlled school pilots, especially independent/public high-school world-language pilots in the DC-Maryland-Virginia area and Korea elementary/middle-school English pilots. It is not yet ready for broad self-serve sales, district-wide procurement, or enterprise-style deployment promises.

Recommended launch motion:

> Founder-led free 2-4 week school pilots with 2-3 teachers per school, selected class levels/classes, manual or Canvas setup, explicit limitation disclosure, weekly feedback loops, and a post-pilot path toward either paid extended pilot or paid school subscription.

## Ready now

- Demo the teacher-controlled assignment workflow.
- Demo student assignment launch and assignment-specific practice.
- Demo class, assignment, and student analytics as instructional signals.
- Demo class join codes for small pilot setup.
- Demo Canvas content and LTI support for Canvas prospects.
- Demo consent-aware voice access and text fallback.
- Demo guardian consent packets and admin compliance rosters.
- Demo Lingual-admin approval and organization lifecycle controls.
- Offer the defined initial pilot: free, 2-4 weeks, 2-3 teachers, selected class levels/classes.
- Discuss post-pilot continuation as either a paid extended pilot or paid school subscription, without quoting fixed pricing yet.

## Sell with caution

- **Canvas integration.** Strong demo point, but manual sync and roster/enrollment limitations must be disclosed.
- **Analytics.** Useful and differentiated, but heuristic. Do not call it certified assessment.
- **Compliance.** Product workflows exist, but legal claims need counsel.
- **School admin controls.** Good beta foundation, not full enterprise administration.
- **Voice practice.** Core value, but raw audio recording/playback and provider-accurate cost metering are not complete.
- **Multi-language story.** The platform supports multiple learning locales, but content depth varies by locale.

## Not ready yet

- Self-serve paid plans.
- Billing and subscription management.
- Enterprise SSO.
- District procurement package.
- Google Classroom integration.
- CSV roster import and bulk/email student invitations.
- Teacher transcript review and audio playback.
- Certified grading or assessment claims.
- Full legal/compliance claim package.
- Formal customer support SLA.

## Biggest business risks

1. **Overclaiming school compliance.**

   The product has consent and deletion workflows, but legal review is still required before making definitive compliance claims. The next legal/procurement research priority is Korean public schools and US public schools.

2. **Selling analytics as assessment.**

   Current analytics are useful instructional signals, but not certified scoring.

3. **Pricing before cost controls.**

   Voice value is high, but provider-accurate usage and cost controls are not complete. Pricing should account for voice-minute exposure.

4. **Too broad a launch motion.**

   The current product is strong enough for supported pilots, not for unsupported self-serve rollout.

5. **LMS expectation mismatch.**

   Canvas is supported, but schools may expect full automated roster, gradebook, and SIS behavior. Sales must scope this carefully.

6. **Teacher onboarding burden.**

   The assignment authoring workflow is powerful, but teachers may need examples, templates, and coaching to create high-quality practice.

## Recommended launch motion

### Primary motion: founder-led school pilot

Use this for the next stage.

- Prioritize independent and public high schools in the DC-Maryland-Virginia area, primarily world-language classes.
- Also target Korea public elementary and middle schools for English-class adoption, with legal/procurement research required.
- Offer free 2-4 week initial pilots.
- Include 2-3 teachers per school.
- Let each teacher choose the class level or class section they want to pilot first.
- Allow all students in the chosen class level or selected classes to use the product during the pilot.
- Require a school-side pilot owner, expected to be a language department chair or administrator chosen by the school.
- Use manual setup, join codes, or Canvas/LTI depending on school readiness.
- Limit promises to beta-supported workflows.
- Run issue triage and weekly email check-ins.
- Collect teacher quotes, outcome signals, and product friction.
- For public-school opportunities, collect surveys and compile a Lingual-side report for district/government buyers before pushing larger adoption.
- For continued use after the first pilot, discuss either paid extended pilot or paid school subscription.

Detailed report design is out of scope for this v1 business package and should be handled later during survey/report planning.

### Secondary motion: department pilot

Good if a language department chair or program lead is the internal champion.

- Package around a small group of teachers.
- Emphasize shared instructional goals and analytics.
- Avoid enterprise procurement claims.

### Do not lead with: broad self-serve launch

Self-serve needs pricing, billing, support, onboarding automation, and clearer upgrade paths. It is not the best first motion.

### Do not lead with: enterprise/district sales

District sales require procurement, legal, security, SSO, contracts, support SLAs, and deeper integrations. These are not current beta strengths.

## Beta success metrics

Use these as pilot learning metrics, not contractual success guarantees.

- Pre-pilot and post-pilot teacher survey feedback.
- Pre-pilot and post-pilot student survey feedback.
- Teacher satisfaction.
- Student satisfaction.
- Number of active teachers.
- Number of classes created.
- Number of students enrolled.
- Assignment creation rate.
- Assignment launch rate.
- Student practice completion rate.
- Estimated speaking minutes.
- Total conversation time.
- Average session chat length.
- Usage yield: how much actual conversation time and session depth the pilot produces from eligible student usage.
- Expansion yield: share of pilot schools that express interest in extended usage after the pilot, measured through direct teacher statements or survey responses.
- AI debrief reporting quality and usefulness for students and teachers.
- Repeat usage by teachers.
- Repeat usage by students.
- Teacher-reported usefulness of analytics.
- Number of consent/support incidents.
- Time from teacher onboarding to first published assignment.
