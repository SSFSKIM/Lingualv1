# Sales FAQ

Status: Business v1 draft
Last updated: 2026-06-01

## Who is Lingual best for right now?

Lingual is best for school language programs that want more student speaking practice but need teacher control, assignment alignment, and consent-aware rollout.

Current segment priority:

1. Independent and public high schools, primarily world-language classes.
2. Language institutes, likely with mixed language offerings.
3. ESL / ELD programs, English-first.
4. Colleges, later phase because the operational and buying system is different.

First outreach targets are independent and public high schools in the DC-Maryland-Virginia area, plus Korea public elementary and middle schools for English-class adoption.

For the school-side owner, expect a dedicated school admin, usually a language department chair or administrator chosen by the school.

Buyer map:

- Independent schools: school administrators and language department leadership.
- Public high schools: language department chairs and school administrators for pilot entry; district-level buyers require more research.
- Language institutions: school or program administrators.
- Korea public elementary/middle schools: district/government buyers are likely the main scale-up path, supported by pilot surveys and Lingual-compiled reports. The Korea public education purchase process needs more research.

## What problem does Lingual solve?

Teachers cannot give every student enough spoken practice during class. Lingual lets teachers design speaking assignments that students can complete at scale, while giving teachers evidence about practice activity and target-language performance.

## What category is Lingual in?

Lingual should be described as an AI speaking-practice platform for schools. The most important market comparison research should focus on school- or institution-used AI-powered conversation-practice platforms, especially B2B products where realtime conversation, speaking practice, or listening practice is central.

## What is the main difference from a generic AI chatbot?

Lingual is assignment-aware and teacher-controlled. The teacher can define the scenario, objectives, target expressions, vocabulary, grammar focus, success criteria, language mix, and notes. Students practice inside that teacher-designed context.

## Can teachers use their existing class materials?

Yes. Current teacher authoring supports Canvas-linked content, pasted source packets, manual advanced inputs, and scaffold-free custom prompts.

Limit: shared curriculum libraries, reusable department templates, and imported curriculum packages are not currently the main beta path.

## Does Lingual support Canvas?

Yes, with limitations. Current Canvas support includes course connection, roster/content sync, content visibility, assignment linking, LTI launch, deep-link assignment embedding, and completion-style grade passback.

Important limits:

- Manual resync, not automatic webhook sync.
- Canvas roster visibility does not automatically enroll students.
- Students still join via code or LTI.
- Grade passback is completion-oriented, not full assessment scoring.

## Does Lingual support Google Classroom?

Not in the current beta.

Status: Planned / Need to be resolved.

## Does Lingual support SSO?

Enterprise SSO is not part of the current beta claim.

Status: Need to be resolved.

## Can students practice by voice?

Yes, when voice is allowed by the student's consent and retention state. If voice is blocked and the teacher enabled text fallback, students can complete assignment-scoped text practice instead.

## Does Lingual store student voice recordings?

Current product state should be described cautiously: raw voice recording storage and teacher playback are not implemented. The product tracks transcribed practice activity and estimated speaking metrics, but teachers cannot currently play back recorded audio.

## Can teachers see student transcripts?

Not yet. Teachers can currently see aggregate activity and learning signals, but full transcript review from the teacher dashboard is not implemented.

## What analytics does Lingual provide?

Teachers can review signals such as sessions, student turns, estimated speaking minutes, words per turn, self-corrections, repeated errors, target-expression usage, task completion, and rubric evidence. Analytics exist at class, assignment, and student drill-down levels.

Important limit: these are heuristic learning signals, not certified assessment scores.

## What is debrief reporting?

Debrief reporting is an upcoming product feature where Lingual's own product AI agent creates debrief reports for students and teachers. It should summarize practice evidence in a readable way, but it should not be sold as certified assessment or a replacement for teacher judgment.

Student-level debrief visibility should be limited to the student and teacher. School admins should not see student-level AI debriefs; their view should stay aggregate unless a later legal/product review changes the sharing model.

## Can Lingual grade students automatically?

Lingual can support completion-style grade passback to Canvas, but it should not be positioned as a certified automatic grading system. Rubric evidence exists, but score calibration and human-equivalent assessment claims are not current.

## Is Lingual compliant with FERPA, COPPA, or state biometric laws?

Lingual has product workflows that support school-safe rollout: consent records, voice gating, guardian packets, retention policies, audit exports, and deletion requests.

However, formal legal validation is Need to be resolved. Sales should avoid making definitive legal compliance claims until counsel has reviewed the workflow and contracts.

The main legal/procurement research scope is Korean public schools and US public schools. Sales should be especially careful with public-sector claims until those paths are researched.

## Can schools delete student data?

The beta includes deletion-request workflows for school admins, including approval, execution, retry, and status tracking.

Limit: deletion execution is beta-level and synchronous. Raw audio cleanup is not currently meaningful because raw audio is not stored yet.

## How long does setup take?

The initial pilot offer is a free 2-4 week pilot for 2-3 teachers. Each teacher can choose the class level or class section where they want to pilot Lingual, and all students in the selected class level or selected classes may use the product during the pilot.

Setup should be positioned as manual setup or Canvas LMS setup, depending on the school's workflow. Exact setup-time promises by integration type are still Need to be resolved.

## Is the pilot free?

Yes. The first pilot should be free. If the school wants continued use after the first pilot, Lingual can discuss either a paid extended pilot or a paid school subscription. Exact pricing and discount policy still require research.

## Can Lingual be sold self-serve?

Not yet as the primary motion. The current product is better suited to founder-led or team-assisted school pilots. A fully self-serve paid motion would require pricing, billing, support, onboarding automation, and clearer procurement policies.

## What pricing plans exist?

Research-deferred.

Current product materials do not define billing, subscriptions, seat limits, school packages, paid voice-minute limits, or renewal workflows. Do not imply fixed pricing exists until business defines it. The current post-free-pilot direction is paid extended pilot or paid school subscription, with public-school scale-up supported by survey evidence and Lingual-compiled pilot reports.

## Who supports the pilot?

Early pilots are founder-led through issue triage and weekly email check-ins. Sales can say that Lingual will support the pilot closely through that channel, but should not promise a formal SLA until the support model is defined.

## What should sales demo first?

Recommended demo order:

1. Teacher dashboard and class setup.
2. Assignment creation from Canvas or source material.
3. Student assignment launch and voice/text practice policy.
4. Teacher analytics: class, assignment, student drill-down.
5. Compliance: consent gating, guardian packet, audit export.
6. Canvas/LTI only if the prospect uses Canvas.

## What should sales avoid demoing first?

Avoid leading with generic free chat, games, avatar features, or pronunciation-only practice. Those may be useful later, but the school pitch should center on teacher-designed assignments and measurable speaking practice.

## What are the strongest objections and answers?

| Objection | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| "We do not want uncontrolled AI talking to students." | Lingual is built around teacher-authored assignment context, not generic free chat. |
| "We need student privacy controls." | The beta includes consent state, voice gating, guardian packets, retention policy handling, audit export, and deletion-request workflows. Legal validation is still required for formal claims. |
| "Teachers do not have time to build lessons from scratch." | Teachers can start from Canvas content or pasted source packets and edit the generated speaking-practice structure. |
| "How do we know students practiced?" | Teachers can see class, assignment, and student-level practice signals such as sessions, speaking time, turns, target usage, and completion. |
| "Can this fit Canvas?" | Canvas/LTI support is current, with known limitations around manual sync and completion-style grade passback. |
| "Can we pilot this now?" | Yes, the initial pilot is free for 2-4 weeks, 2-3 teachers, and the selected classes/class levels they choose. |
| "Can we buy this now?" | The post-pilot direction is paid extended pilot or school subscription, but exact pricing, contract terms, and public-school procurement path still require research. |
