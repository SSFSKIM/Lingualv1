# Priority Competitor Tear-Downs

Status: Research draft
Last updated: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This document profiles the competitors and adjacent products most relevant to Lingual's school-facing positioning.

The goal is not to copy competitor features. The goal is to understand what buyers may already expect, where competitors are strong, where they are weak, and how Lingual should respond.

This document separates two research layers:

1. **Positioning and messaging.**
   How each competitor describes itself, which buyer pain it emphasizes, what category language it uses, and what trust signals it leads with.

2. **Product specifics.**
   What the product appears to do in practice: speaking mode, teacher workflow, reporting, integrations, pricing, compliance posture, and procurement path.

## Executive Summary

The priority competitor set is converging around one buyer problem:

> Students need more speaking practice than teachers can manually deliver or review.

But each competitor claims a different territory:

- **Plang School** owns Korean English process assessment and teacher workload language.
- **LG CNS** owns Korean public-sector AI English infrastructure language.
- **EBS AI PengTalk** owns Korean public elementary AI speaking legitimacy.
- **AI Digital Textbook vendors** own curriculum/textbook distribution.
- **Speakable** owns district-ready speaking/writing assessment workflow.
- **Speakology AI** owns AI avatar conversation and oral-practice messaging.
- **Telo AI** owns ELL 1:1 AI companion language.
- **Extempore** owns oral assessment, language-lab, and department alignment.
- **Flowent/Talio** own scalable speaking practice for language programs.

Lingual should not lead with generic "AI conversation practice." The more defensible position is:

> Teacher-controlled conversation assignments for language classrooms, aligned to curriculum objectives and producing school-usable speaking evidence.

## Product Tear-Downs

### Plang School

Positioning and messaging:

- School-facing message: English process-centered assessment made easier.
- Consumer Plang message: AI English speaking app that trains users to think and speak in English.
- Buyer promise: reduce English teacher workload while giving students AI feedback and evidence-generating practice.
- Sales motion: school page with teacher login, student login, free-start, and adoption inquiry; education-office adoption signals.

Product specifics:

- Buyer: Korean schools, teachers, academies, and education offices.
- Target segment: English classes needing writing, reading, speaking, listening, and process assessment.
- Capabilities: AI + teacher feedback, handwriting scoring, real-time quizzes, reading scaffolding, speaking pronunciation/content feedback, listening assessment, student evidence.
- Reporting: student activity evidence and student-record-style comments.
- Integrations: public signals around Seoul AIEP, Jeonnam `아이모두`, and Gyeonggi Hi-Learning contexts; Canvas/Google/Clever/LTI not found.
- Pricing: school pricing not public; education-office funded/free access reported for Jeonnam 2026.
- Compliance/procurement: strong public-school signal, but CSAP/ISMS-P, raw audio handling, and school-specific DPA posture need resolution.
- Lingual response: do not pitch as an AI English speaking app in Korea; pitch teacher-controlled conversational assignments tied to curriculum objectives, pilot reports, and safe data handling.

Sources: [Plang School](https://edu.plang.ai/school), [Plang](https://www.plang.ai/), [Plang AI technology](https://www.plang.ai/ai-technology), [Plang Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=co.aienglish), [Seoul contract report](https://m.dhnews.co.kr/news/view/1065573661439195), [Jeonnam contract report](https://www.beyondpost.co.kr/view.php?ud=2026050418260366779aeda69934_30), [Dogung Elementary privacy policy](https://dogung-e.goegh.kr/dogung-e/iv/indvdlView/selectIndvdlView.do).

## Cross-Competitor Patterns

1. **The category is splitting into practice, assessment, and infrastructure.**
   Speakology, Flowent, Talio, Telo, and Nualang focus on practice. Speakable, Extempore, and Plang emphasize assessment/evidence. LG CNS, EBS, and AIDT vendors emphasize infrastructure or public-sector channels.

2. **Teacher workflow is becoming the real battleground.**
   Most credible competitors do not only promise student practice. They promise teacher setup, teacher review, dashboards, rubrics, reports, or easier assessment.

3. **Korea requires local school vocabulary.**
   Plang's strength is that it speaks in Korean school terms: process-centered assessment, performance assessment, student evidence, and student-record comments.

4. **US products make procurement readiness visible.**
   Speakable, Telo, Extempore, and similar tools lead with privacy, LMS, SSO, DPA, purchase-order, or standards-alignment signals.

5. **Pronunciation/scoring is no longer defensible by itself.**
   ELSA, Plang, LG CNS, Speakable, and others already offer feedback/scoring language.

6. **The best competitors turn practice into evidence.**
   Lingual's output must be useful to teachers after the session, not only engaging during the session.

## Lingual Response

Lingual should lead with:

> Teacher-controlled AI conversation assignments that help students speak more, stay aligned to curriculum objectives, and produce class-ready evidence after every pilot.

Sales-safe current claims:

- Teacher-led pilot scope.
- Speaking-time and usage reporting.
- Student and teacher survey loop.
- AI debrief as an upcoming student/teacher evidence layer.
- Manual setup or light LMS setup for scoped pilots.
- Founder-led support.

Claims that need product/legal proof before sales uses them:

- District-ready deployment.
- Full FERPA/COPPA compliance.
- Korea public-school procurement readiness.
- AI Digital Textbook equivalence.
- Full LMS/SSO/rostering support.
- Admin-level reporting beyond aggregate pilot reports.

## Positioning Gaps For Lingual

Open positioning territory:

- **Korea:** "Curriculum-aligned AI conversation assignments for English class, producing speaking evidence teachers can use for process-centered assessment."
- **US high school/world language:** "Teacher-controlled AI speaking practice that creates measurable speaking time and curriculum-tied debriefs."
- **US ESL/ELD:** "Safe, school-managed AI speaking practice for English learners, with teacher visibility and pilot reporting."
- **Language institutes:** "Scalable AI conversation practice that extends teacher capacity without replacing teacher control."
- **College/language programs:** "Instructor-visible speaking practice with cohort evidence and curriculum-aligned scenarios."

Territory to avoid:

- Generic AI chatbot.
- Cute elementary AI companion.
- Pronunciation-only coach.
- Assessment-only recorder.
- Avatar-first novelty product.
- Full LMS/textbook replacement.
